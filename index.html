Aims & Scope

The STA Journal of Student Research (SJSR) is a multidisciplinary, student-ran publication dedicated to showcasing the original research, reviews, and scholarly work of students at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School. Our mission is to provide a platform where students can explore their academic interests, engage in critical inquiry, and share discoveries with a broader community of peers, educators, and researchers.

The journal accepts submissions from all fields of study, including but not limited to:

  • Natural Sciences, Mathematics, and Medicine
  • Social Sciences and Humanities
  • Engineering, Technology, and Computer Science
  • Economics, Business, and Finance
  • Arts, Literature, and Creative Scholarship

The Journal invites original research papers and review articles that are not published or being considered for publication elsewhere. The Journal publishes new issues twice a year, in January and July.

Submission Guidelines

Eligibility

  • Any currently enrolled student at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School.
  • All work must be completed independently, outside of standard classwork or graded assignments.
  • Students may choose to work with a mentor (teacher, university professor, professional, etc.), but mentorship is not required.
  • Students may work individually or in groups (working with those not enrolled at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School is acceptable).

What We Accept

We currently accept:

  • Original Research.
  • Review Articles.
  • Creative Scholarship.

What We Do Not Accept

If any of these things are submitted, it will be automatically be rejected:

  • Class projects, lab reports, or papers submitted for a school grade.
  • Plagiarized or AI-generated content.
  • Previously published work in another journal or magazine (including online platforms with formal publication).

Submitting Your Manuscript

Manuscripts should be prepared as word/google documents in doc/pdf format only. All submissions should be emailed to editorial.sjsr@gmail.com. The author(s) may state preference for single column or doulbe column. However, the final format will be at the editorial's discretion

How to Prepare Your Manuscript

All manuscripts need to include:

Title Page

The first page of a manuscript is the title page which should contain: title of the manuscript, authors' full names, affiliations of all authors, and information for correspondance.

  • The title of the manuscript should be clear, concise and informative.
  • Provide the name(s) of authors as First Name (Given name) -Middle Name- Last name (Family name/Surname) for each author and check that all names are accurately spelled.
  • Present the author's affiliation (academic institution, company, etc) below the names of authors. Indicate all affiliations with superscript letter immediately after the author's name and in front of the appropriate affiliation address.
  • Clearly indicate who will handle correspondence at all stages of refereeing and publication, and post-publication.
  • Provide the e-mail address of each author.

Abstract

The second page of manuscript should contain the abstract and keywords. The abstract should be informative, and summarize the paper. It is recommended that you write this last.

  • The abstract should not more than 250 words in length.
  • Abstract should briefly present the purpose of the paper, state the scope of the experiments (if any), indicate significant results, and summarize major findings and conclusions.
  • An abstract is often presented separately from the article, so it must be able to stand alone. Therefore avoid references/citations in the abstract.
  • Provide four-six keywords that relates to the paper.

Introduction

This should be state the objectives of the work and provide an adequate background. Introduction should clearly state the hypothesis or purpose statement, how and why the purpose or hypothesis was developed and why the author thinks it important.

Conclusion

This should clearly explain the main conclusions of the work highlighting its importance and relevance. It should be short and concise but should not repeat the abstract. Generally, the conclusion does not cite references.

References: Modified Vancouver Style

Refernces should be cited in the order they appear in the text. Source(s) should be indicated as [number] in the order they appear in-text. For exmaple, "... which is supported by previous studies [4, 5], which found..." and "... previous research conducted only examine idealized scenarios [13-16]." The actual authors can be referred to, but the reference number(s) must always be given: for example, 'Shug et al. [8] obtained...'

It is highly recommended that references are articles from other academic journals, texts, etc.

For the references page, please refer to this link. Make titles of works in title case.

Acknowledgements

All acknowledgments (if any) should be included at the very end of the paper before the references. No authors should be mentioned here.

For Research Articles, Include:

Methodology/Materials and Methods

This section must contain specific details about the materials studied, instruments used, and related experimental details which allows other researchers to reproduce the results. Obtain permission for all fully borrowed, adapted, and modified tables and provide credit in the footnote. If any human study was carried out, a written consent should be taken from the human subjects involved in the study ensuring their acceptance in the study and publication of relevant datas/photos/images in the journal.

Results and Discussion

The results should be concisely presented. Results and discussion may be separate or combined based on the author's discretion. Tables and figures should be designed to maximize the comprehension of the experimental data. The interpreted results should be explained clearly in discussions and should relate them to the existing knowledge in the field as clearly as possible. Tables, graphs and figures (illustrations) should be inserted in to the main text at respective place they should appear when published and should have appropriate numbers and titles with an explanatory heading. Labels of the table, graph and figures must be in the text form and should not form part of the image.

For Review Articles:

Organization of the review article is at the author's discretion, but must include all require sections.

Join Us

Interested in being part of the editorial team? The STA Journal of Student Research is currently looking for new editors! If you're interested in editing and publishing research, we'd love to hear from you.

To apply: Email your name, grade, and a brief statement of interest to editorial.sjsr@gmail.com. Make sure to mention which discipline you're applying for and attach any relevant work or experience.

Job Description

  • Reviews submissions in a specific academic discipline.
  • Provides subject-specific feedback and helps make publication decisions.
  • Uses LaTeX to edit and format manuscripts prior to publication.

Requirements

  • Must be a currently enrolled student at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School.
  • Must have published a related article with the Journal in the subject they wish to edit.
  • Strong academic background for the subject is recommended.
  • Strong academic writing, attention to detail, and commitment to deadlines are essential.

Applications will be considered by other members of the editorial team.
