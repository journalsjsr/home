Welcome to Our Journal!

The STA Journal of Student Research (SJSR) is a research journal open to all fields of study. Our mission is to showcase original thinking, inquiry, and exploration by students at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School. Whether you're conducting scientific experiments, analyzing historical events, exploring literature, or diving into computer science, we welcome your work.

Submission Guidelines

Eligibility

  • Any currently enrolled student at St. Thomas Aquinas Regional Secondary School.
  • All work must be completed independently, outside of standard classwork or graded assignments.
  • Students may choose to work with a mentor (teacher, university professor, professional, etc.), but mentorship is not required.
  • Students may work individually or in groups.

What We Accept

We currently accept:

  • Original Research Articles.
  • Review (Literature Review) Articles.

(Please see Article Types for more details on each.)

What We Do Not Accept

If any of these things are submitted, it will be automatically be rejected:

  • Class projects, lab reports, or papers submitted for a school grade.
  • Plagiarized or AI-generated content.
  • Previously published work in another journal or magazine (including online platforms with formal publication).

Acceptable Article Types

1. Research Articles

Research articles present original work conducted by the student(s). These may include experimental data, observational studies, theoretical work, surveys, or creative approaches to research in any academic discipline. For more information, please download this document.

2. Review Articles (Literature Review)

Review articles summarize and synthesize existing research on a specific topic. Rather than presenting new data, these articles should offer insight into trends, gaps, or debates in the field. For more information, please download this document.

Available Roles & Requirements

Editor

  • Edits accepted articles for grammar, clarity, and coherence.
  • Ensures articles meet SJSR formatting standards.
  • Must be comfortable using LaTeX for formatting research papers.
  • Attention to detail and strong writing/editing skills required.

Section Editor

  • Reviews submissions in a specific academic discipline (e.g. physics, psychology, history, etc.).
  • Provides subject-specific feedback and helps make publication decisions.
  • Must have completed or currently taking (with 86%+ average) at least a Grade 12 or AP-level course in the subject area they wish to edit.
  • Should be familiar with academic research and terminology in their field.

Reviewer

  • Reads and assesses submissions for originality, clarity, and academic rigor.
  • Works with editors to recommend acceptance, revision, or rejection.
  • Must have strong reading comprehension and critical thinking skills.
  • Prior research experience is an asset, but not required.
